Oromiya

Oromia is the largest regional state in Ethiopia, spanning a massive land area of approximately 353,690 square kilometers, which translates to roughly 353,690,000,000 square meters. This vast region features incredibly diverse landscapes that range from the rugged peaks of the Bale Mountains to the fertile valleys of the Great Rift Valley system. It serves as the primary homeland of the Oromo people, the largest ethnic group in Ethiopia, and acts as the economic backbone of the nation by driving agricultural production, particularly through the cultivation of coffee, grains, and livestock.
